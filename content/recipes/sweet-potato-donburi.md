Roast vegetables: does it get easier than that? Pour'em on a cookie sheet, shove'em in the stove, pretend you have a knack for it. Call it _donburi_ for that magical Japanese vibe.


## Ingredients {#ingredients}

|              |              |
|--------------|--------------|
| Green Onions | 2 stalks     |
| Sweet Potato | 1            |
| Lime Juice   | A splash     |
| Broccoli     | 1 small head |
| Eggs         | 2            |
| Sauce        |              |
| Rice         | 1/2 cup      |
| Water        | 3/4 cup      |


## Instructions {#instructions}

1.  Move oven rack to top and preheat to 425 degrees
2.  Clean and prepare veggies
    1.  Thinly slice onions, separating greens from whites
    2.  Peel sweet potato and cut in half lengthwise
    3.  Cut sweet potato crosswise into half-moon shapes
    4.  Cut broccoli into bite-sized broccoli pieces
3.  Start the rice

    1.  Heat a bit of oil in a small saucepan over **medium** heat
    2.  Soften the onion whites ~1 minute, till the kitchen begin to smell nice
    3.  Add rice, 3/4 cup water, and a nice pinch of salt
    4.  Boil, cover, then reduce to a simmer
    5.  Cook till tender, **15-18 minutes**

    **NOTE:** you can also just do this in the rice-cooker, but on the stove, it's a lot easier to make less rice
4.  Roast the veggies
    1.  Cover a cookie sheet in parchment paper
    2.  Put the sweet potato on one side, the broccoli on the other
    3.  Toss both of them in oil, salt, and pepper
    4.  Put in the oven **15-18 minutes**
5.  When the veggies are almost done, start frying two eggs. Sunny side up with a crunchy bottom fits in nice
6.  Put it all together
    1.  Rice in bowl first, then vegetable then eggs
    2.  Top it all off with lime juice and a sauce (see notes below)
    3.  Finally, sprinkle on the green parts of the onions


## Notes {#notes}


### On sauces {#on-sauces}

The hello fresh I stole this from used a honey miso sauce that was basically 97% percent prepared. Once I figure out a way to replicate it, I'll make a post for it as well, but until then, here are some sauces I think fit with this ok enough:

-   Tonkatsu sauce: a lil sweet
-   Sweet Chili sauce: sweet as well, but that little bit of peppery heat almost makes up for the lack of flavor balance
-   Hot sauce: It goes on everything. It goes on everything. It goes on everything.
