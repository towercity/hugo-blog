A recipe that makes only two servings! With the tempeh though, and the trips to
more bougie grocery stores it requires, makes this a bit expensive though. One
of many travails of attempting to eat vegetarian.


## Ingredients {#ingredients}

|                 |                             |
|-----------------|-----------------------------|
| Tempeh          | 8oz block, in 1 inch slices |
| Soy sauce       | 1/4 cup                     |
| Sugar           | 3 tbsp                      |
| Hot Sauce       | 1 tbsp                      |
| Lime juice      | 1 tbsp                      |
| Cooking Oil     | 1 tbsp                      |
| Salt and pepper |                             |
| Fresh ginger    | 2 inches, peeled and minced |
| Garlic          | 1 clove / 1 tsp             |
| Green Onion     | 2, thinly sliced            |
| Rice            | Cooked                      |


## Instructions {#instructions}

1.  Make rice
2.  Prep ingredients
3.  Simmer a fair amount of water in fry pan **with lid** over **medium heat**
4.  Add tempeh to pan and simmer, covered, for ~10 minutes
5.  Move tempeh from pan with tongs to cool
6.  Rinse fry pan
7.  In **small bowl**, whisk soy sauce, sugar, hot sauce, and lime
    juice
8.  Dry tempeh, crumble it
9.  Heat oil in fry pan over **medium-high heat**
10. Saute tempeh til lightly brown, ~3-4 minutes
11. Add salt and pepper, ginger, and garlic; cook like 30 second: till
    fragrant
12. Pour whisked sauce in the pan and cook till thicker and darker:
    about 6-7 minutes


## Notes {#notes}


### 2021-08-25 {#2021-08-25}

**Big:** Make sure to add a good amount of water to the tempeh to steam it. I
didn't today, and it burnt a little to the bottom of the pan.

Also, this was waaay too gingery. Maybe halve the ginger next time, to see how
well that works.
