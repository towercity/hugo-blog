A very creamy, tomato-y sauce. Very delicious, but also unkind to stomachs.


## Ingredients {#ingredients}

|                 |                            |
|-----------------|----------------------------|
| Olive Oil       | 2 tbsp, or really whatever |
| White Onion     | 1, Diced                   |
| Garlic          | 1 Clove / 1 tsp            |
| Diced Tomatoes  | 14.5oz, undrained          |
| Dried Basil     | 1 tbsp                     |
| White Sugar     | 3/4 tsp                    |
| Dried Oregano   | 1/4 tsp                    |
| Salt and Pepper |                            |
| Heavy Cream     | 1/2 cup                    |
| Butter          | 1 tbsp                     |


## Instructions {#instructions}

1.  In the biggest pot, start boiling your pasta water. Make it as you
    go, right
    -   this'll vary based on the pasta, of course. This assumes a 10-15
        minute pasta shape, not angel hair or similar
2.  Chop onions/garlic; mix together the sugar and all the spice
3.  In the mid-sized saucepan, saute onion and garlic in olive oil over
    **Medium Heat** until soft and delicious-smelling. Mmmm.
4.  Add the tomaoes and the seasoning mix now
5.  Heat the sauce until it boils
6.  Boil 6-7 minutes, or till most of the tomato liquid evaporates
    -   this is a good time to prep the cream and butter
7.  Reduce heat to simmer, add cream and butter
8.  Simmer 5 minutes
    -   Depending on where pasta is, start on the dishes now.
9.  pour the sauce into the blender, blend on "Sauce"
    -   Open the steam hatch after, just in case
    -   Taste again after blending: it'll be a bit more bland
